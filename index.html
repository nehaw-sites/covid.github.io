Symptoms

Coronavirus What It Is?

01

Fever

A fever is a body temperature that’s higher than is considered normal. It’s also called a high temperature. It’s usually a sign that your body is working to keep you healthy from an infection. Normal body temperatures are different for everyone, but they lie within the range of 97 to 99. A temperature of 100.4 or higher is considered a fever.

02

Cough

Cough is your body's way of responding when something irritates your throat or airways. An irritant stimulates nerves that send a message to your brain. Brain tells muscles in your chest and abdomen to push air out of your lungs to force out the irritant. A cough that persists for several weeksmay indicate a condition that needs medical attention.

03

Tiredness

A Fatigue is a feeling of constant Tiredness or weakness and can be physical, mental or a combination of both. Tiredness can affect anyone, and most adults will experience fatigue or tiredness at some point in their life. Each year, around 1.5 million Australians see their doctor about fatigue. A tiredness or fatigue is a symptom, not a condition.

How To Protect Yourself

  • Wear Mask
  • Wash Your Hands
  • Stay At Home
  • Social Distance
  • Use Sanitizer

Some ways for protection from COVID-19

Official Public Service Announcement on Coronavirus from Official WHO website. Learn about Symptoms, Prevention, etc. & Common FAQs on Covid-19.

• Washing your hands frequently with soap or hand wash.
• Staying home as much as possible, even if you don’t feel sick.
• Avoiding crowds, gatherings, all non-essential shopping & travel.
• Keeping 6 feet of distance between yourself & others when out.

Wear Mask

Here are the basics of how to wear a mask:

Clean your hands before you put your mask on, as well as before and after you take it off, and after you touch it at any time. Make sure it covers both your nose, mouth and chin. When you take off a mask, store it in a clean plastic bag, and every day either wash it if it’s a fabric mask, or dispose of a medical mask in a trash bin. Don’t use masks with valves.

Wash Your Hands

When and How to Wash Your Hands?

Touch your eyes, nose, and mouth with unwashed hands. Prepare or eat food and drinks with unwashed hands. Touch a contaminated surface or objects. Blow your nose, cough, or sneeze into hands and then touch other people’s hands or common objects.

Stay At Home

Why do we stay at home?

Fewer people you’re around, lower your chance of infection. When you stay home, you help stop the spread to others, too. Don’t go to crowded places. If your community is under shelter-in-place orders, follow proper guidelines for when you can leave your home. Keep a stock of supplies like food and medicine, so you can hunker down when you need to.

Social Distance

How to follow social distancing?

Keep Distance at Events and Gatherings: It is safest to avoid crowded places and gatherings where it may be difficult to stay at least 6 feet away from others who are not from your household. If you are in a crowded space, try to keep 6 feet of space between yourself and others at all times, and wear a mask.

Use Sanitizer

Uses of sanitizer:

Sanitizer acts by killing certain germs on the skin. Although alcohol-based hand sanitizers can quickly reduce number of germs in many situations, they should be used in right situations. Soap & water are more effective than hand sanitizers at removing certain kinds of germs like norovirus, Cryptosporidium & Clostridioides difficile, as well as chemicals.
